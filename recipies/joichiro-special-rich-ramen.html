Joichiro Special Rich Ramen

Description

Joichiro is a breakfast ramen dish. It is unusual to make ramen for breakfast. This ramen dish is relatively light, making it perfect for breakfast. It looks like it has meat, but ideally, it is meatless. Instead, the Joichiro use tempeh, a meat substitute. Tempe goes well with miso soup-based vegetarian broth and soy milk. The dish also has dried shiitake mushrooms that bring out meaty umami, some potatoes, and vegetables. It has ginger, garlic, and seasonings that make it tremendously delicious. Because of the tempeh, the ramen ends up having a meaty flavor without all the heaviness. As a result, it is revitalizing and energizing. This makes it a perfect dish to start your day with.

Ingredients

  • Ramen noodles
  • Grilled Carrots
  • Grilled Turnips
  • Rock salt
  • Yuzu citrus
  • Tempeh
  • Soy sauce
  • Sake
  • Vegetable broth
  • Shitake mushrooms
  • Kelp
  • Soy Milk
  • Miso
  • Ebi-imo-taro
  • Fried gobo root
  • Fried lotus root
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Chilli oil

Steps

  1. Make the buddist stock. Put Kombi seaweed, Shitake Mushroom and water into a pot.
  2. Turn Stove on to medium heat. When the water is just about to boil, remove the kombu. Let it boil for two minutes, then remove from heat and strain.
  3. Make toppings. Thinly slice the lotus root, leaving the skin on. Use a peeler to make thin strips of burdock. Fry in oil until crispy and then sprinkle with rock salt
  4. Cut carrots into sticks. Cut turnips into bite sized pieces. Heat the sesame oil in a frying pan and saute both until golden brown. Sprinkle with rock salt.
  5. Make the teriyaki tempeh. Cut the chunks into 5cm pieces. Sprinkle with 1 tablesoon of sake and grated ginger and then lightly coat with potato starch.
  6. Heat sesame oil in a frying pan and saute step 5 until thoroughly cooked. Add Soy sauce and mirin, coating the tempeh well
  7. Make the broth. Put miso, ginger, and garlic into a pot and sear lightly. Add the soy milk and "buddist" stock and stir until the miso is dissolved. Grate the ebi taro into the pot and let it for 5 min. Simmer with salt and pepper to taste
  8. Finishing touches. Boil the ramen noodles in a separate pot then strain and shake off any extra moisture. Put cooked noodles in a bowl and pour step 7 in. Top with 3, 4, and 6. Drizzle with chilli oil and done.
